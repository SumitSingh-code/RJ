Layer 2: Why Harmonic Mean?
Combining two directions honestly — penalizing one-sided matches
2ab / (a + b)

We need ONE number from two scores. Why not just average them?

Normal Average
70

(90 + 50) / 2

vs
Harmonic Mean
64

2 x 90 x 50 / 140

Why This Matters

If Person A scores 90 and Person B scores 50:

  • Normal average says 70 — sounds okay
  • Harmonic mean says 64 — more honest

A match where one person is very happy and the other is not — that is not a good match. Harmonic mean reflects this reality.

Vivaan x Tara: Real Output

Vivaan → Tara: 78.4
Tara → Vivaan: 82.4
Harmonic Mean: 80.3
2 x 78.4 x 82.4 / (78.4 + 82.4) = 80.3

Layer 3: Gale-Shapley
Nobel Prize-winning algorithm (2012) — finds globally stable matching

What Does "Stable" Mean?

A matching is stable when no two unmatched people would both prefer each other over their assigned partners.

How It Works (Simple)

  • Step 1: Every person ranks all opposite-gender candidates
  • Step 2: Males propose to their top choice
  • Step 3: Females accept the best, reject the rest
  • Step 4: Rejected males propose to next choice
  • Step 5: Repeat until everyone is matched

"Mathematically guaranteed — no two people will want to leave their partners for each other."

Top Pick is NOT Always Stable Match

Vivaan's Results

Top Pick: Tara (80.3)
Highest individual reciprocal score

Stable Match: Meera
Best globally stable assignment

Vivaan prefers Tara, but someone else also prefers Tara AND Tara prefers them more. So globally, Vivaan gets Meera — his best possible stable partner.

Our app honestly shows both — your personal top pick AND your stable partner — and explains the difference.
